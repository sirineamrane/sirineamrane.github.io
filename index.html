About

More about me

I'm Sirine, a Computer Science student with a focus on machine learning systems and optimization, currently completing my BSc at the University of Hertfordshire.

I'm passionate about the inner workings of things: the gears, the architecture, the underlying logic. For me, Computer Science has always been an academic discipline in the truest sense, much like philosophy. Not just a craft, but a field grounded in deeper theoretical thinking, mathematical insight, and systems-level reasoning.

Understanding a system properly means holding two lenses at once: a micro view (the low-level mechanics, the primitives, how things actually behave under the hood) and a macro view (the emergent behaviour, the design decisions, the architecture that shapes everything above).

I thrive on collaboration and real challenge. The kind of projects that push my understanding, stretch my limits, and let me contribute, however incrementally, to something bigger. We're heading into an era of accelerating technological change, and I intend to be part of what comes next.

Machine Learning for Clinical Decision Support in Pediatric Care

An ongoing research project at CHU Mustapha Bacha (Algiers), Pediatric Service, applying machine learning to predict patient response to a gluten-free diet in pediatric celiac disease. Conducted in collaboration with an Assistant Professor in Pediatrics, the project explores how predictive models can support clinical decision-making in a real-world hospital environment.
